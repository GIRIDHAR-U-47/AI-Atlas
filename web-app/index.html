Adversarial Search

In complex environments, an agent often faces an opponent whose goals are in direct conflict with its own. This is the domain of adversarial search.

Definition

A zero-sum game is a mathematical representation of a situation in which each participant's gain or loss is exactly balanced by those of the other participants.

Minimax Strategy

The minimax algorithm is a decision-making tool used for finding the optimal move in a zero-sum, symmetric game. It works by minimizing the possible loss for a worst-case scenario.

Consider the logic of alpha-beta pruning. By avoiding the exploration of branches that cannot influence the final decision, we significantly increase search efficiency.

The Calculus of Conflict

Modern AI research extends these classical search patterns into high-dimensional latent spaces, where "opponents" may be simulated environments or generative adversaries.
